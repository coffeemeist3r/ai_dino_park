# Cycle 108 — Lore Handoff

**Theme:** Someone's name gets said. Milestone 6 has spent four cycles building a park where food moves —
banked (446), spent (444), ferried across an edge (447), and last cycle it finally grew **hands**: the dino
that keeps putting food away where it lives emerges as the `provider` 🧺 (448). But a role nobody talks about
is a database column. Every other standing this park has invented — the gossip, the quiet hero (260), the
hoarder — earns its weight the moment a *second* dino says it out loud. **453 closes the milestone's lore
checklist by letting the park name its provider:** a dino that meets another, or greets the keeper, can let
the pantry-keeper's standing slip into its line — *"the Fernreach eats because of Sunny"* — so the first
economic role in the park stops being a tag on a lens and becomes something the residents *know*.

The read we want: you walk into a zone you haven't visited in a while, greet the first dino you find, and it
tells you who's been feeding the place. You didn't open a lens. Somebody told you.

**Suggested next-up (lore track):** **BACKLOG-453 — Word of the provider.** It is the only unchecked M6
lore arc, and its blocker cleared last cycle when 448 shipped the durable `provider` role tag. Two things
to hold the Designer to:

1. **A provider must be nameable by someone who isn't it.** The line belongs in the *witness's* mouth. A
   provider bragging about itself is a different (weaker) beat; the arc's phrasing — "the Fernreach eats
   because of Sunny" — is third-person on purpose. Reputation is what others say when you're not the one
   talking.
2. **Voice it per temperament, not per template.** This park has spent cycles 55–58 learning that the same
   debt said in five voices is the whole point (253/261/262). A warm dino should credit the provider
   generously; a prickly one should concede it grudgingly; the *fact* stays identical either way. Sameness
   across dinos is the bug (CHARTER "Living minds").
