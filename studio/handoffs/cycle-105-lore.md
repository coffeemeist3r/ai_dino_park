## What this beat should *feel* like

The park already has three separate things this needs and has never joined: a dino can be
**withdrawn** (374's moping loner), a dino can **cross the bowl for a friend** (130's comforting
nuzzle), and food **arrives at a place** (the hatch drop). 381 is the wiring, and the wiring is
the whole point — the friend does not comfort in place, it *leads*. The read the player should
get, in order:

1. Food drops. Most of the cast turns toward the hatch. One dino at the edge does not.
2. Its closest friend — and it must be its **closest**, not the nearest body — breaks off from
   its own beeline to the food and goes the *wrong way*, out to the edge.
3. A nudge beat, then the pair moves toward the hatch together. The loner is following, not
   being teleported; the friend arrives second because of the detour.
4. Both dinos remember it. The loner's memory ("Twitch came and got me") should be the kind
   that can colour the next thing it says to the keeper, the way "woke hungry" does now.

**The failure mode to design against:** this must not fire every drop, and it must not fire for
a loner that was already heading in. A dino that's merely *far from the hatch* is not withdrawn.
The gate belongs on the withdrawn state (374) plus a real bond floor (136's "comfort is for
friends" shape) — a near-stranger doesn't come. If nobody clears the bar, nobody comes, and the
loner misses the meal. That silence is the feature; it's what makes the fetch mean something
when it happens.

**Distinctness hook:** *who* comes is a read on the bond graph, and *whether anyone comes* is a
read on whether this dino has anyone at all. A loner with no bond above the floor (135) stands
at the edge while the park eats. That's the same cruelty the cold-night arc earned its weight
from, and it should read just as plainly.
