# Cycle 25 — Lore Handoff

**Theme:** Feeding day. The bowl has a label, a glass you can tap, a little sun — now it needs a *hatch in the lid*. The keeper's most basic act of keeping is dropping food in and watching who scrambles. This is the next fishbowl beat (BACKLOG-059): reframe "gifting" — a deliberate hand-to-one-dino act — as **feeding the whole tank**, where the keeper picks the *input* (when, where food drops) and the bowl decides the *outcome* (which dino, by temperament and where it stood, snaps it up). The swarm is pure emergence: the eager rush, the calm shrug, one wins, the rest disperse. Lean into the cretaceous-aquarium feel.

**Added to BACKLOG:**
- BACKLOG-061 [emergent] Food favorites — each dino has a preferred food (derived from its personality, like gifts); a dino that grabs its favorite at the hatch reacts extra-happy (bigger affinity bump, 😋 vs 🙂) and remembers it. Builds on 059.
- BACKLOG-062 [emergent] Scramble standoff — when two dinos reach dropped food on the same tick, the bolder one wins and eats; the loser sulks (memory + a brief 😤) and a low-bond pair loses a little bond. Emergent conflict over scarcity.
- BACKLOG-063 [social] Begging at the glass — a dino that hasn't been fed in a long while drifts to the front wall and looks up at the keeper (📣/👀 over its head), nudging the player to drop food. A want the bowl expresses on its own.
- BACKLOG-064 [emergent] Hoarder role — a dino that wins the food scramble most often emerges as the `hoarder` role tag (joins gossip/homebody/socialite/wanderer), derived purely from feed tallies.
- BACKLOG-065 [pokemon] Feeding log in the book — the collection book gains a per-dino "fed Nx · last Day M" line, so the keeper can see who's thriving and who's going hungry.

**Suggested next-up:** **BACKLOG-059 — the feeding hatch.** It's the spine the whole 061–065 cluster hangs off (favorites, scramble, begging, hoarder role, feed log all assume a feeding mechanic). Ship the hatch + swarm this cycle; the rest become emergent layers on top.
