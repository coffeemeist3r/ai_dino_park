## Lore-track pick — BACKLOG-367 "The food web wakes" (+ folds in 435, its data spine)

The last lore arc of Milestone 3 and the marquee emergent beat: **the bowl's first hunt.** A hungry
carnivore stalks the nearest herbivore, which flees. **Deathless** — no catch-kill, no mortality (that
stays a CHARTER call routed to the operator, per the cycle-80 hunting split). The hunt is a *pursuit*,
not a death: the predator closes, the prey bolts, and when the stalker reaches it the quarry **escapes**
(💨) and the hunt comes up empty, leaving both a small remembered trace. Twitch — the jittery compsognathus,
the roster's only theropod — becomes the lone hunter; the four big herbivores are the hunted. Distinctness
through diet: the bowl finally has a predator *and* prey, and who is which reads at a glance the moment
hunger bites.

367 is blocked on **BACKLOG-435 (the diet split)** — the roster has no diet, so there is no predator/prey
read to stand on. Rather than burn a cycle shipping the data alone, **435 folds into this arc as its data
spine** (arc-sized rule, v6): the diet field + FOODS `kind` tag ship *with* the hunt that consumes them.
435 stays a clean data layer (no behavior change of its own — herbivores don't yet refuse meat at the
hatch); only 367 acts on it. This closes both 367 (lore) **and** 435 (folded), draining the Structure
Track by one as a side effect.

**Why the fold, not two cycles:** the food web is one coherent playable arc — a diet read is meaningless
until something hunts on it, and the hunt can't exist without the read. Splitting them ships a crumb
(a data field nothing uses) and a blocked arc. Together they ship *the first hunt*, end to end.
