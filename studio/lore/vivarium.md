# The Vivarium — the fishbowl frame

*Established cycle 22 (2026-05-30), at the operator's prompt: "treat this like a mini dino fishbowl."*

## The premise

The world is not a park the player walks through. It is a **sealed glass vivarium** — a pocket Cretaceous on a shelf. A self-contained little biosphere, kept and observed, never escaped.

The dinos do not know they are inside anything. They wake with the bowl's small sun, wander the grass, meet, bond, nest at the den, hatch eggs, carry gossip, settle into roles, and grow families — entirely within the glass.

**The player is the keeper, looking in.** Not an avatar in the scene so much as a presence at the glass: you tend, you feed, you watch, you occasionally tap. You do not author what happens. The bowl runs itself; that is the whole point.

## Why it fits what we already built

The fishbowl framing is not a pivot — it is the name for what the emergent stack already is:

- **The emergent sim** (meet → bond → huddle → breed → gossip → roles) is the bowl living on its own. "Leave one prompt, come back to a whole world" becomes literal: come back and peer into the glass.
- **The observer lenses (V)** are ways of pressing your face to the glass — the collection book, the bond threads, the role tags, the news ticker.
- **Gifts** are food dropped through the feeding hatch.
- **Day/night** is the bowl's sun on a timer.
- **The bounded 20×15 map** was always the glass wall. We just named it.

## Design rule (unchanged)

Show a mirror, never author an outcome. The keeper nudges *inputs* (food, a tap on the glass, a placed decoration) and never dictates *outcomes* (no scripted romances, no forced events). The dice stay in the sim's hands.
